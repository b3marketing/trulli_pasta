Empty enriched flour

Many brands that sell pasta in the United States use enriched flour as their base ingredient. Enriched flour has been refined and bleached so much that it is devoid of nearly all of its nutritional value. This refinement increases the shelf-life and gives breads and pastas a finer texture but creates a bland, tasteless, and unhealthy outcome. Some, but not all, of the nutrients that are stripped from this flour are added back through enrichment and not in the same amounts. It also doesn’t include the fiber and protein that are present in the discarded parts of the wheat berry.

The way your body processes enriched flour is a problem too. Whole flours that contain the whole wheat berry are digested slowly and contain fiber and nutrients to help maintain blood sugar. Enriched flour is quickly broken down and converted to sugar, giving a quick energy burst. Any excess sugar after it has been metabolized is stored as fat. Our whole wheat pasta is rich in protein, fiber, and B vitamins and may support weight loss, heart health, and digestion.

Semolina, Durum Wheat and Whole Wheat

Semolina Flour is a product of coarsely grinding Durum Wheat. Durum Wheat is the second most cultivated wheat species following bread wheat, also called common wheat. It is grown in the hot, dry environment surrounding the Mediterranean Sea and harvested in the fall. Durum wheat can be ground into Semolina, a course flour used to make pastas and couscous, or used to make cereals, puddings, bulgur or ground further to make bread or pizza dough. Whole wheat is similar to Durum wheat in that it uses the whole wheat berry but is made from the much more popular bread wheat.

When ground into Semolina, the harder Durum wheat berry has some of its starch content damaged making it less suitable for bread. Additionally, Durum lacks the D genome in common wheat that makes it’s dough a good choice for bread-making. Semolina, on the other hand, has higher extensibility, meaning it can be more easily stretched into long pieces without breaking.

Pasta made from whole wheat Semolina has a richer texture and flavor profile, it keeps you full longer and provides a wide range of essential vitamins and minerals in addition to being a great source of fiber and protein.
